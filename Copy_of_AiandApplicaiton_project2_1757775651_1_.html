E-Commerce Shopping Behaviour Analysis¶

1. Business Overview¶

Our client is an E-commerce company that sells a variety of products across different categories such as clothing, footwear, and accessories. With increasing competition in the online shopping industry the company wants to better understand customer behaviour in order to

  • Improve sales and marketing strategies
  • Optimize discounts, promo codes and subscription programs
  • Personalize product recommendations
  • Identify seasonal and regional shopping trends

About the Dataset¶

the dataset contains 3900 customer records with 18 attributes that describe demographics, purchase details, and shopping habits. key features include:

  • Customer demographics: Age, Gender, Location
  • Purchase details: Item, Category, Size, Color, Season, Purchase Amount
  • Behavioural data: Frequency of Purchases, Previous Purchases, Subscription Status
  • Promotional factors: Discounts, Promo Codes
  • Transaction details: Payment Method, Shipping Type
  • Feedback: Review Ratings

Why This Analysis?¶

As data scientists, our task is to help the company gain actionable insights from this dataset. Through Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), we will uncover trends such as:

  • How age, gender, and location influence shopping preferences
  • Which product categories are most popular during different seasons
  • The effect of discounts and subscriptions on spending behaviour
  • Customer loyalty patterns and payment preferences

These insights will support data-driven decision-making, allowing the company to increase customer satisfaction and maximize revenue.

# 1. Handle Missing Values

# Strategy:
# - For Age → fill with median (better than mean, less affected by outliers)
# - For Purchase Amount → fill with median
# - For Review Rating → fill with mode (most common rating)

df['Purchase Amount (USD)'].fillna(df['Purchase Amount (USD)'].median(), inplace=True)
df['Review Rating'].fillna(df['Review Rating'].mode()[0], inplace=True)

#Question 1 -How does Age influence customer spending (Purchase Amount)?



# Create Age Groups for better comparison
bins = [0, 18, 25, 35, 50, 65, 100]
labels = ['<18', '18-25', '26-35', '36-50', '51-65', '65+']
df['Age Group'] = pd.cut(df['Age'], bins=bins, labels=labels, right=False)

# Average spending per age group
age_spending = df.groupby('Age Group')['Purchase Amount (USD)'].mean().reset_index()

# Plot the result
plt.figure(figsize=(8,5))
sns.barplot(x='Age Group', y='Purchase Amount (USD)', data=age_spending, palette='viridis')
plt.title("Average Purchase Amount by Age Group")
plt.ylabel("Average Purchase Amount (USD)")
plt.xlabel("Age Group")
plt.show()

Final Conclusion and Suggestions

Overall Strengths of the Analysis¶

  • We explored 7 diverse business questions covering demographics, shopping behaviour, promotions, payment methods, and locations.
  • The dataset allowed us to see clear patterns in spending, preferences, and loyalty behaviours.
  • Visualizations helped translate raw data into actionable business insights.

Limitations¶

  • Dataset size is relatively small (3,900 records). Larger data may reveal stronger patterns.
  • Some columns had missing values, duplicates, or inconsistencies, which we cleaned — but in real scenarios, more advanced methods may be required.
  • Data only covers limited attributes; external factors (like festivals, holidays, income levels) are not included.

Key Insights & Recommendations¶

  1. Age & Spending:
    Customers in the 26–35 age group spend the most.
    👉 Focus marketing and premium offers on this segment.

  2. Gender Preferences:
    Males and females show different category preferences.
    👉 Run gender-targeted ads (e.g., fashion for women, footwear/electronics for men).

  3. Seasonal Trends:
    Certain categories peak in specific seasons (e.g., clothing in summer, jackets in winter).
    👉 Adjust inventory and seasonal promotions accordingly.

  4. Discounts & Promo Codes:
    Discounts do not always increase spending — sometimes customers just buy cheaper items.
    👉 Use promotions strategically, not for every product.

  5. Subscription Program:
    Subscribers show higher purchase frequency and spending.
    👉 Invest more in subscription/loyalty programs to boost retention.

  6. Payment Methods:
    Some payment methods are more popular (e.g., Credit Card, PayPal).
    👉 Prioritize seamless checkout on these methods, but also encourage underused ones with offers.

  7. Locations:
    A few regions generate the majority of sales.
    👉 Focus marketing and logistics on top-performing regions; push awareness in weaker ones.

Final Recommendation¶

The company should:

  • Target 26–35 age group with premium products.
  • Personalize marketing by gender and season.
  • Grow subscription programs to lock in loyalty.
  • Optimize promotions to avoid unnecessary discounts.
  • Strengthen operations in high-sales regions while boosting campaigns in low-performing ones.

By acting on these insights, the company can increase revenue, improve customer satisfaction, and strengthen loyalty.
